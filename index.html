Applied Projects

Design Thinking Course Project

FOCUS – Collaborative Learning Platform

Problem Statement

Students struggled with burnout, lack of interaction, and fragmented notes during the pandemic.

Execution

  • Empathize: Conducted interviews with students (grades 6–10). Captured motivations using empathy maps and personas.
  • Define: Insight: students lacked structured, peer-supported learning. Framed as: How might we help students learn collaboratively despite remote constraints?
  • Ideate: Brainstormed study groups, mentorship, and skill-sharing. Value props: qualified tutors, reliable notes.
  • Prototype: Built low-fidelity app screens (login, dashboard, interest selection).
  • Test: Simulated user scenarios mapped using a full customer journey. Collected feedback on usability.

Outcome

The project demonstrated the power of peer-led learning and inspired later work on collaborative academic platforms.

Entrepreneurship + Design Thinking

STRIDE – Automated Interview Preparation Tool

Problem Statement

Students lacked accessible, structured preparation for behavioral and soft-skills interviews.

Execution

  • Empathize: Conducted 40 interviews (students & recruiters). Insight: need for mock interviews and feedback.
  • Define: Persona: 19–24-year-olds. Core needs: convenience, targeted practice, feedback.
  • Ideate: Explored AI-based mock interviews, structured feedback, expert interviews.
  • Prototype: Developed functional prototype using Python-based NLP models. Built flows for mock interviews.
  • Test: Validated assumptions around technology adoption and usefulness. Iterated based on early testers.

Outcome

Demonstrated high demand and produced strong early traction metrics—320 potential paid users during placement season projections.
